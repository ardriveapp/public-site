# Introduction

ArDrive is proud to introduce its new "Licensing" feature, designed to empower users with greater control and flexibility over the distribution and use of their uploaded files. This guide provides an in-depth overview of the licensing options available, including the Universal Data License (UDL) and a suite of Creative Commons licenses. By understanding and utilizing these licensing options, you can ensure that your work is shared and used in a manner that aligns with your preferences.

# Licensing Your Content

When you upload files to ArDrive, you now have the option to apply a license after. This feature is vital for content creators, educators, and anyone interested in specifying how their work can be used by others. Licensing your content can protect your rights while also enabling you to contribute to the global commons of knowledge and culture.

## How to Apply a License

1.  **Upload Your File:** Begin by uploading your file(s) to ArDrive as you normally would.
2.  **Assign License:** After the upload process, select the file and apply a license.
3.  **Select a License:** You can select from the Universal Data License (UDL) or any of the Creative Commons licenses.
4.  **Review and Confirm:** After selecting a license, review the details to ensure it aligns with your intentions for the file. Once confirmed, the license will be applied to your file.

At this time, there is no additional cost to apply a licenses files on ArDrive.

## Viewing Licenses

Anyone accessing your files on ArDrive can view the license you've applied by viewing the file's details in the right panel. This transparency ensures that users understand the permissions and restrictions associated with a file, whether accessing an individual file or the full drive.

# Understanding Your Licensing Options

## Creative Commons Licenses

Creative Commons is a globally recognized nonprofit organization that offers a standardized way to grant public permission to share and use creative work. By applying a Creative Commons license to your files, you specify the freedoms and limitations others have in using your work. This clarity benefits both creators and users, facilitating the sharing and use of copyrighted material in a legal and straightforward manner.

### The Six License Types

Creative Commons offers six main license types, ranging from the most permissive (allowing for both commercial use and modifications) to the most restrictive (only allowing others to download your works and share them with others as long as they credit you, but they can’t change them in any way or use them commercially). Additionally, the CC0 Public Domain Dedication allows creators to waive all rights and place a work in the public domain.

Learn more from [Creative Commons](https://creativecommons.org/share-your-work/cclicenses/) about these licenses, as well as CC0 Public Domain Dedication.

### Choosing the Right License

Selecting the appropriate license is crucial. We recommend using the [Creative Commons License Chooser](https://chooser-beta.creativecommons.org/) tool, which guides you through the process of selecting a license that aligns with your intentions.

## Universal Data License (UDL)

The Universal Data License is specifically designed for content stored on public permissionless blockchain networks. It is a legal framework that outlines the terms of use for your content, addressing the unique considerations of blockchain-based storage. The UDL is an excellent choice for users who wish to leverage the security and transparency of blockchain technology for their content.

[Learn more](https://dev.to/fllstck/the-universal-data-license-explained-2di) about the Universal Data License and how it works.

## Impacts of Licensing

Applying a license to your content has several implications:

-   **Legal Clarity:** Licenses provide legal clarity about what others can and cannot do with your content, protecting your rights while facilitating its use.
-   **Control:** You maintain control over how your content is used and distributed.
-   **Contribution to the Commons:** By selecting a license that allows for sharing and reuse, you contribute to the global commons, supporting a culture of collaboration and innovation.
